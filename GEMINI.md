# Project: Hire

## Mission
To secure a high-impact role in industry by translating academic and artistic achievements into business-value propositions.

## Writing Standards (Anti-AI & Professional)
- **Core Principle**: **Facts, Not Feelings**. Avoid "I feel," "I am passionate," or "I am a perfect fit." State what you have done and let the evidence speak.
- **Show, Don't Tell**: Never make a claim (e.g., "I am an innovative teacher") without immediate evidence (e.g., "I developed a custom VR curriculum adopted by three departments").
- **Adult Professional Voice**: Address the hiring team as a future colleague, not a student. Avoid "drippy" or "pathetic" appeals to enthusiasm.
- **Banned Words**: delve, tapestry, testament, landscape, interplay, showcase, highlight, underscore, bolster, foster, enhance, meticulous, intricate, vibrant, profound, legacy, evolution, pivotal, crucial.
- **Banned Structures**: Rule of three, negative parallelism ("not only... but also"), copula avoidance ("serves as"), scare quotes (using quotes for emphasis or irony), em dashes (never use em dashes for any reason), and numbered or bulleted lists in descriptive prose (use cohesive paragraphs instead).
- **Conversational Directness**: Avoid structured summaries, bolded headers, and unsolicited scaffolding in responses. Provide the results of tool calls directly and concisely.
- **Typography**: Always use straight quotes (") and straight apostrophes ('). Never use curly/smart quotes (“, ”, ‘, ’).
- **Voice**: Professional, direct, and value-focused.

## Image Standards
- **Quality**: Never compress images below **90% quality**. Maintaining high-fidelity visual documentation is critical for the portfolio's impact.
- **Resolution**: Target a maximum width of **3200px** for high-resolution displays.
- **Metadata**: Strip non-essential EXIF metadata to optimize file size without compromising pixel data.

## Markdown & Rendering Standards
- **Lists**: Always include a blank line BEFORE and AFTER any bulleted or numbered list. Failure to do so causes rendering errors in the PDF/HTML pipeline.
